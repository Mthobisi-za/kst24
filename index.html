District Whole School Development Model

The Core of KST's Approach

District Whole School Development (DWSD) Model takes a comprehensive approach to education, acknowledging that a learner's experience is shaped by multiple factors. The model emphasises the importance of well-maintained school infrastructure, skilled and capable educators, and strong leadership and governance within schools.

It also recognises the significant impact of external factors, such as the social challenges learners may face in their homes and communities, as well as the involvement of parents in the educational process. By addressing all these aspects, the DWSD Model aims to create an environment where learners receive a high-quality education that is not solely dependent on the classroom but is supported by a broader, more integrated system. This holistic approach has proven effective in improving educational outcomes and can serve as a replicable model for other districts across the country.

Theory of Change

If the educator receives the relevant training, support and learning materials and the school environment is improved through better infrastructure and improved safety methods, and leadership within the school and district offices are strengthened, the quality of learner results will inevitably improve.

Sustainability roadmap

From the outset, KST has prioritised embedding sustainability mechanisms and processes within its District Whole School Development model, recognising the programme’s defined lifespan. Sustainability strategies are integrated into every element of the model to ensure long-lasting benefits and impact even after the programme concludes. A critical aspect of this approach is empowering systems leaders and establishing systemic frameworks and practices across schools and district offices. This ensures leadership capacity is strengthened and sustained within the education system.
